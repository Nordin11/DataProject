Motive

Investing in stocks is becoming increasingly popular. Due to the complexity, this space is quite inaccesible for people without a financial background. There are no clear connections between the stocks and it seems to behave quite randomly.
With this product the bridge is built over the knowledge gap by giving a go to insight in the financial system and trigger the user to do further reading on the findings attained from this product.

The network

The network serves as a overall view over the system (25 stocks, 5 industries). In this visualization the reading can see the correlation* lines of the stocks in general, not in detail. The lines are green when the correlation is positive and red when negative. With the radius of the nodes the user can see which of the companies has the biggest sum of correlations, by this the user can conclude which company has the most influence in the system. With the slider you can change from which correlation you want to see the lines. For example when the slider is in the middle at 0.5, the user only sees the lines that have a correlation greater than 0.5 or -0.5. This visualization has to serve as a helicopter view over the system.

The correlation matrix

The correlation matrix gives a little bit more detailed information. By clicking on an option in the dropdown menu the user can zoom in at the chosen industry by seeing the correlation matrix of the industry itself. This serves as an indicator in how the industries work between themselves.

The line graph

The line graph gives the user an insight in the average price change of the two stocks that have a correlation. By clicking on a cell in the correlation matrix the user gets a line graph of the two stocks that affiliate with that cell. By this the user gets another zoom in with more detailed information and more clarity in where this correlation comes from.

Remember!

This product doesn't serve as investment advise. It just tries to serve as a tool for gaining more insight in the financial system. You have to make your own conclusions and decisions.


* Correlation refers to how close two variables (in this case stocks) are to having a linear relationship with each other.

Click on an industry in the dropdown menu to show correlation matrix.
Click on a cell in the correlation matrix to show the line graph.
